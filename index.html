Past Projects:

My Github is the best place to find my work, but you can find further information on my more discursive projects here.

  • Hestia - A Hierarchical Storage Manager (HSM) for hetereogeneous devices - see more:

    • Hestia's role in the IO-SEA project was/is to provide an efficient storage manipulation API, which provides an event feed of storage events allowing a "policy manager" (based on CEA's Robinhood engine) to manipulate data across the tiers exposed by Hestia. The policy manager will use the feed to ensure the data are in the optimal locations for user-end applications on target cluster. My initial focus in Hestia was in the DevOps of the project, overhauling this completely to provide a more robust continuous integration toolchain which fully utilizes Gitlab's CI toolkit and containerized workflows, and well as providing a new, automated testing and deployment workflow. Following this I implemented a first draft of Hestia's event feed, and presented this to stakeholders and collaborators for feedback. I helped in using this feedback to create the feature's second revision. Participated in the target-driven testing and review for delivering Hestia's first full release. Re-worked the CI system once more as the complexity of the project's needs grew into a re-usable and testable Python module for CI operations. Additionally, I took advantage of time provided by ICHEC to pursue development of my other skill sets by taking part in EuroCC-run workshops. Notable mentions include a multi-day workshop on the topic of distributed machine learning techniques in a HPC context and and introduction to the use of the ParaStation software suite.

About me

Caelen joined Partec-AG in November 2023 as a Software and Architecture Development Engineer. She works primarily in DevOps and integration of the ParaStation Software suite with production systems.

Caelen joined ICHEC Dublin in April 2023. At ICHEC, she was involved in developing software for hierarchical storage management (HSM). She participated in completing the IO-SEA WP4 Deliverable 4.3, working on the HSM API Hestia.

Prior to joining ICHEC, Caelen worked on research projects in HPC, mathematical modelling (of EEG signals and PDEs), quantum computing, machine learning, and numerical computation of complex functions. This research was conducted with EPCC/PRACE, Trinity College Dublin, Equal1, Ausar Geophysical and TCD respectively between 2016 and 2022.

She completed her undergraduate degree with Trinity College Dublin in 2020. She enjoys working on community outreach/educational projects, such as her work with the WEEArchie Raspberry Pi HPC mini-cluster and several years of teaching extracurricular mathematics classes on weekends for secondary school students in the Junior Mathematics Enrichment Program of UCD. In the wake of these experiences, she is interested in establishing solutions that allow for accessible and effective education a society dealing with the impacts of COVID.

Caelen is an avid reader of fiction, enjoys board games and peaceful walks. She can juggle 3 objects quite well, and 4 quite poorly. She is currently learning the constructed language Toki Pona, and re-learning Irish - má mhaith leat caint as Gaeilge leí.
